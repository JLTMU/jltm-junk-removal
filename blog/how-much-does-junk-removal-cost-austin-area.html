How Much Does Junk Removal Cost in the Austin Area?

March 9, 2026 • 7 min read

If you've ever tried to get a straight answer about junk removal pricing, you know how frustrating it can be. Most companies won't give you a number until they're standing in your driveway. Some advertise "prices starting at $99" but never tell you what that actually gets you.

We think that's backwards. You should know what you're going to pay before you commit to anything. So here's a real breakdown of what junk removal costs in the Austin area — and what affects those numbers.

What Affects Junk Removal Pricing?

1. Volume

This is the biggest factor. A single couch costs less than a garage full of stuff. Most companies estimate volume by looking at what you have and comparing it to their truck capacity.

2. Item Type

Some items require special handling or disposal:

  • Appliances with freon (fridges, ACs): +$25-50 per unit for proper refrigerant disposal
  • Mattresses: +$15-35 each due to disposal regulations
  • Tires: +$10-25 per tire
  • Heavy materials (concrete, dirt, roofing): +$100-200 per ton
  • Construction debris: Sometimes priced higher due to disposal costs

3. Location

Companies based in downtown Austin often charge fuel surcharges for suburbs like Leander, Cedar Park, and Georgetown. Some add $25-50+ for driving out to Williamson County.

That's why we based JLTM in Leander — no fuel surcharges for our service area.

4. Labor Difficulty

If your junk is on the third floor of a walk-up, in a backyard accessible only through the house, or requires disassembly, expect to pay more. Some companies charge hourly labor on top of the haul fee.

The Hidden Fees to Watch For

Not all junk removal pricing is apples-to-apples. Watch for:

  • Fuel surcharges: Common for Austin companies servicing the suburbs
  • "Disposal fees": Sometimes added on top of the quoted price
  • Labor charges: Hourly rates for heavy or difficult items
  • Stairs fees: Some companies charge per flight of stairs
  • Minimum charges: That "$99" price is often a minimum that covers almost nothing
At JLTM, our prices are all-in. The price we quote is the price you pay. No surprises.

How to Get the Best Value on Junk Removal

1. Get multiple quotes

This is standard advice for a reason. Prices vary significantly. Just make sure you're comparing apples to apples — ask about surcharges and fees.

2. Consolidate your junk

If you can pile everything in one spot (the garage, the driveway), the job goes faster and sometimes cheaper.

3. Do what you can yourself

If half your stuff could go in the regular trash with some effort, do that and save the junk removal for the things you can't handle alone.

4. Ask for a photo quote

Many companies (including us) will give you an estimate based on photos. This saves everyone time and eliminates the "bait and switch" when they arrive.

5. Consider timing

End of month (when leases turn over) and spring cleaning season are busy times. If your job isn't urgent, scheduling for a slower period might get you faster service.
