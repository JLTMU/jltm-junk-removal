If you've lived in Leander for any length of time, you've probably accumulated more stuff than you realized. The garage that was supposed to hold two cars now holds zero. The spare bedroom became a storage room. That pile in the corner of the yard has been there since the last round of landscaping.

Getting rid of it all feels overwhelming — but it doesn't have to be. Here's everything Leander homeowners need to know about junk removal: what it costs, what we take, and how to make the process as painless as possible.

What We Take (And What We Don't)

The short answer: we take almost everything. The slightly longer answer:

Things we haul every day:

  • Furniture (couches, mattresses, tables, chairs, desks, bookshelves)
  • Appliances (refrigerators, washers, dryers, water heaters, dishwashers)
  • Yard waste (branches, brush, leaves, old fencing)
  • Construction debris (drywall, lumber, flooring, tiles)
  • General household junk (boxes, old décor, exercise equipment, toys)
  • Garage contents (tools, storage bins, random accumulated stuff)

Things we can't take:

  • Hazardous chemicals (paint thinner, pesticides, pool chemicals)
  • Propane tanks and pressurized containers
  • Car batteries
  • Medical waste and sharps
  • Asbestos or asbestos-containing materials
  • Electronics (TVs, monitors, computers) — contact us for e-waste referrals

If you're not sure whether we can take something, just ask. We'll tell you straight.

How to Prepare for Junk Removal Day

You don't need to do much — that's the whole point of hiring us. But a few things can make the process faster and smoother:

  1. Decide what goes. Walk through the space beforehand and make your decisions. The crew shouldn't be waiting while you debate whether to keep that lamp.
  2. Clear a path. If we're emptying a garage, make sure we can get to it. If there's a gate, make sure it's unlocked.
  3. Point out the heavy stuff. Let us know if there's a piano, a safe, or a concrete birdbath. We can handle it, but it helps to know in advance.
  4. Secure pets. We'll be going in and out — best to keep dogs and cats in another room.

What to Expect on Pickup Day

Here's how a typical JLTM job works in Leander:

  1. We confirm. You'll get a call or text with our arrival window. We show up when we say we will.
  2. We assess. We look at everything that needs to go and give you a final price based on volume. If you already got a photo quote, this just confirms it.
  3. You approve. Once you say yes, we get to work. You don't lift a finger.
  4. We haul. Everything goes into our truck and trailer. We sweep up after ourselves.
  5. You pay. Cash, card, or digital payment — whatever works for you.

Most single-item pickups take 15-20 minutes. A full garage cleanout might take 1-2 hours. Either way, you get your space back the same day.
